Data sources & how it works

Where the roster comes from

The full unit list and each unit's element are pulled from your Grand Summoners Tier List sheet (master data tab, released units only). That's — units.

Tiers — imported ✓

Real tiers (SS → F, including +/−) are scraped from the grandsummoners.info Global Tierlist and matched to the roster by name + element (—). Your sheet's own tier tab is image-based and can't be read as text, so this text tierlist is used instead. Any unmatched unit or disagreement can be fixed per-unit with the ✎.

Roles & traits — imported ✓

Rather than loading 440+ pages, all unit skill/arts/passive text was read from the site's single data bundle, and role + traits (arts battery, paralysis, freeze, burn, mitigation, cleanse, true damage…) were derived from that text (—). Role is a heuristic — a unit that mainly deals damage but also grants party arts is called an Attacker, one whose battery + utility dominates is a Support, party-healers are Healers, taunts are Tanks. Anything that looks off is a one-click ✎ fix, and your edits always win.

Equipment, slots & sources — imported ✓

The full equipment list (—) — type, rarity, ATK/HP/DEF, effect, and source (main story stage, side story, event, boss/raid, dungeon, craftable, arena or gacha banner) — was pulled from the same bundle, with effect-based tags derived automatically. Each unit's 3 equip slots and their types are also in (—). In tab 2 you can mark which equips you own (click a row); once you do, the Team Builder only suggests equipment you have. The Priorities tab surfaces vital unowned equips to farm and exactly where they drop, and a Build ▶ button loads any fight straight into the Team Builder. Owned equipment is saved and included in 💾 Save.

Main Quests & Giant Bosses

Main Story is listed chapter by chapter (Chapters 1–38; Global is at Ch.38 per the site changelog). Story stages mix elements and there is no published per-stage element table anywhere, so chapters are treated as mixed-element: the builder recommends your highest-tier balanced team rather than an element counter.

Giant Bosses — the hardest content players actually struggle on — are fully modelled from the community Giant Boss guide: all 18 (Degilmagna, Oldeus, Fosradius, Elpneumas, XTF Eraser, Freydal, Flogassa, Valzendeth, Ifrit, Palamicia, Aerugraxes, Tonetaurus, Mavroskia, Ragsherum, Goldola, O'zagun, Wilkes…). Each boss's element is derived from its resistance weakness (a "-X% Earth Res" boss is weak to Earth, so it's a Water boss), and its counter-needs (paralysis, burn, freeze, break, mitigation, cleanse) come straight from its mechanics — so the Team Builder picks a real counter team and the Priorities tab points you at the right farm equips. Note: the game has no public per-EX-stage table; these Giant Boss raids are the equivalent hard fights.

Team Builder logic

Each owned unit is scored for the selected fight: element advantage (Fire→Earth→Water→Fire, Light↔Dark = +20% dmg / −20% taken), matching the boss's curated counter mechanics (paralysis vs Palamicia, burn vs Aerugraxes' freeze-regen, Breaker + true damage vs break-only Ragsherum) — these are the only hard requirements — plus an owned True Weapon bonus (⚔️ tag it via ✎). Tier is a tiebreak only, capped so it can never outrank a required counter mechanic — a correctly-tagged C-tier Breaker beats an off-kit SS nuker on a break-only boss. Slots are then filled by marginal team gain: stacking the counter element compounds elemental buffs/debuffs, supports whose element matches your damage dealers get a synergy bonus (element-locked buffs actually land), and stacking multiple units weak to the boss is penalized. Arts battery and healer are soft coverage preferences, not forced slots — because auto-derived trait tags are imperfect, forcing a "battery" could displace a stronger unit, so a battery/healer is preferred only when it doesn't cost you a better pick. Equipment suggestions respect each unit's per-slot star cap (set real caps via ✎), so you're never told to slot gear the unit can't hold. Reasoning is shown per pick.

Data freshness

Tiers, kits, equips and images were last refreshed on —. A GitHub Action (.github/workflows/update-data.yml) runs daily: it pulls the latest data from the grandsummoners.info source repo, appends any new units/equipment, rebuilds the tier list, validates everything, and commits — so the site updates itself. New units get auto-derived roles/traits (heuristic); per-unit ✎ edits always override snapshot data and survive updates via 💾 Save.

Priority logic

Your roster is checked for element coverage, roles and key traits. Each fight gets a readiness score (can you counter its element? do you own its required traits?) weighted against its value. Content you can clear floats up; blocked content shows exactly what to acquire next.
